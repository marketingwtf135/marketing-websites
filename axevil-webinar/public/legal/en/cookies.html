Cookie Policy

Last updated: 18 May 2026

This Cookie Policy describes the cookies and similar technologies that Axevil Capital, LLC uses on this webinar registration website (the “Site”) and how you can manage your preferences. It should be read together with our Privacy Policy.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your browser when you visit a website. We also use similar technologies such as pixels, web beacons and local storage. For brevity, all of these are referred to as “cookies” in this policy.

2. Categories of cookies we use

2.1 Strictly necessary (always on)

These are required to make the Site work — for example to remember your cookie consent choice, to submit the registration form, and to keep the Site secure. The Site will not function correctly without them.

2.2 Analytics

Used to understand how visitors find and use the Site so we can improve it. These are only loaded after you accept analytics cookies in the consent banner. Examples: Google Analytics 4.

2.3 Advertising and conversion measurement

Used to measure the performance of our paid campaigns on Google, Meta (Facebook / Instagram), LinkedIn and Telegram, and to show you relevant Axevil ads on other websites. These are only loaded after you accept advertising cookies. Examples: Google Ads conversion tag, Meta Pixel, LinkedIn Insight Tag.

3. Third-party cookies

Some cookies are set by third parties whose services we use (for example Cloudflare for performance and security, Google for analytics and ads, Meta and LinkedIn for advertising). These third parties may process data outside your country. Please see their own privacy and cookie policies for details.

4. Managing your consent

When you visit the Site for the first time, a consent banner asks you to accept or reject non-essential cookies. You can change your choice at any time by clearing the “cookie-consent” entry from your browser’s local storage and reloading the page, or by using the privacy controls in your browser.

You can also manage cookies directly in your browser settings — most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. Note that blocking strictly necessary cookies may prevent parts of the Site from working.

5. Do Not Track

We respect Global Privacy Control (GPC) signals where technically feasible. We do not currently respond to legacy “Do Not Track” browser signals because there is no industry consensus on how to interpret them.

6. Changes

We may update this Cookie Policy when the technologies we use change. The “Last updated” date above reflects the latest version.
